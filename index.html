01 — Brand Identity

The Soul
of the Brand

Nicole Covello Therapy occupies a rare space: the intersection of warmth and intellectual depth. This is not a clinical, sterile practice. It is a living, breathing invitation — an environment where people who have always held it together feel safe enough to finally let go.

The visual identity reflects this through an earthy, grounded warmth. Deep terracotta and rust carry courage and honesty. Muted steel blue brings calm, perspective, and openness. Peach-cream grounds everything in softness. Together they say: you are held here.

Typography pairs a classical display serif (Cormorant Garamond) used at large scale with clean uppercase Raleway for navigation and labels — creating a contrast between timeless depth and modern clarity. The overall effect is editorial without being cold; spiritual without being ambiguous.

Brand Pillars
"When we pause to listen inward — beyond the noise — we may find that what we've been seeking has been within us all along."
Courageous Welcome
Every person who lands here deserves acknowledgment. The brand meets them with warmth before anything else.
Intellectual Depth
Nicole's approach is nuanced, relational, and grounded in theory. The brand communicates seriousness without severity.
Human Complexity
The gray is celebrated here. Contradictions are welcomed. The brand never oversimplifies healing.

04 — Components

UI Library

Every interactive element follows these exact patterns. No new button styles. No new field treatments. Extend, don't invent.

Buttons

Rules:
Pill radius (100px) on all buttons. Uppercase Raleway 12px. Primary = rust bg. Ghost = white border, used on dusk or rust backgrounds only. Never use more than one primary CTA per viewport.

Form Fields

Dusk blue (#7FA8C4) fill, no border. Labels in white Raleway 500 uppercase. Rust section background. No rounded pill — use 6px radius.

06 — Layout Rules

Spacing & Structure

The layout must feel unhurried. Generous whitespace signals that the visitor is not being rushed. These rules are non-negotiable.

Margin & Padding

Mobile: 24px horizontal padding. Desktop: clamp(56px, 8vw, 120px). Vertical section padding: 80px mobile / 120px desktop. Never compress sections to fit more content.

Max Width

Content max-width: 1100px, centered. Body copy max-width: 640px. Never let body copy span full width on desktop — it breaks readability and warmth.

Color Rhythm

Sections alternate: Cream → Rust → Dusk → Rust → Cream → Rust. Never two cream sections adjacent. Never two rust sections adjacent without a dusk or transition break.

Wave Transitions

Curved SVG wave dividers are used between contrasting section colors (cream-to-rust, dusk-to-rust). The wave curves downward into the next section color. Single gentle arc only.

Image Treatment

Photos always in cards with border-radius 16–32px. Portrait images use the arch frame. Texture/nature images (sand swirl, sphere, mountains) bleed to edges within their section containers.

Mobile Priority

The site was designed mobile-first and should feel perfect on mobile. All grids collapse to single column. Navigation is hamburger-only on mobile. Hero text stacks above the CTA with adequate breathing room.

08 — Brand Standards

Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do

  • Use Cormorant Garamond for all large display type — it is the brand's soul
  • Let the rust section backgrounds carry weight — they signal depth and intentionality
  • Use generous whitespace between all elements — the site should never feel crowded
  • Write body copy in all-caps Raleway on rust backgrounds — it mirrors the site's voice: clear, direct, warm
  • Use the wave SVG divider to transition between sections — it's the brand's signature structural element
  • Let images breathe — always pair photos with ample surrounding space
  • Write with empathy and specificity — copy should speak directly to the visitor's internal experience
  • Keep the color palette to the 7 defined colors — never introduce new brand colors

✗ Don't

  • Don't use more than one primary CTA button per viewport section
  • Don't place body text at full desktop width — max 640px for readability
  • Don't use Cormorant Garamond below 36px — it loses its grace at small sizes
  • Don't use the dusk blue as a text color on white — contrast is insufficient
  • Don't introduce photography of strangers without explicit brand approval — imagery should feel editorial and grounded
  • Don't use gradients — the palette transitions are handled via wave SVGs and section blocks, not linear gradients
  • Don't use drop shadows on text or UI elements — they conflict with the flat, warm aesthetic
  • Don't use clinical or sterile language — always warm, always human, never jargon-heavy
