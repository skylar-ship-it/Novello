01 — Brand Identity

The Soul
of the Brand

Nicole Covello Therapy occupies a rare space: the intersection of warmth and intellectual depth. This is not a clinical, sterile practice. It is a living invitation — an environment where people who have always held it together feel safe enough to finally let go.

The visual identity reflects this through earthy, grounded warmth. Deep terracotta and rust carry courage and honesty. Muted steel blue brings calm and perspective. Peach-cream grounds everything in softness. Together they say: you are held here.

Cormorant Garamond does almost all the heavy lifting — used at every scale from headline to body. Its classical elegance communicates timelessness, depth, and a quiet authority that never feels clinical.

"When we pause to listen inward — beyond the noise — we may find that what we've been seeking has been within us all along."
Courageous Welcome
Every visitor deserves acknowledgment before anything else. The brand meets them with warmth first.
Intellectual Depth
Nicole's approach is nuanced and relational. The brand communicates seriousness without severity.
Human Complexity
The gray is celebrated here. Contradictions are welcomed. The brand never oversimplifies healing.

06 — Layout & Spacing

Space & Structure

The layout must feel unhurried. Generous whitespace signals the visitor is not being rushed. These constraints are non-negotiable.

Horizontal Padding
Mobile: 28px. Tablet: 48px. Desktop: clamp(56px, 8vw, 120px). Never compress sections to fit more content.
Vertical Rhythm
Section padding: 88px vertical minimum. Between body elements: 20–28px. Between sections: no margin — background color handles separation.
Max Widths
Content container: 1100px centered. Body copy: 600px max. Never let paragraphs span full desktop width.
Color Rhythm
Cream → Rust → Dusk → Rust → Cream → Rust. Never two creams adjacent. Wave SVGs handle all color transitions.
Border Radius
Cards & images: 16–32px. Buttons: 100px pill. Form fields: 6px. Arch frame: CSS border-radius arch formula. Never mix radius families.
Mobile Priority
All grids collapse to 1-col. Nav is hamburger-only. Hero type uses clamp(). Buttons go full-width. Test on 390px viewport.

08 — Brand Standards

Do's & Don'ts

Do
  • Use Cormorant Garamond for everything — headlines, body, nav, quotes. It is the brand's primary voice.
  • Use Raleway only for buttons, eyebrow labels, and form field labels. Nothing else.
  • Let rust sections breathe — no cramming. Long copy is a feature, not a problem.
  • Use the wave SVG between every contrasting section. It's the brand's signature structural element.
  • Write body copy in all-caps Cormorant 500 on rust sections — it mirrors the philosophy pages exactly.
  • Keep body paragraphs under 600px wide at all times.
  • Write with empathy and specificity. Copy speaks directly to the visitor's inner experience.
  • Test on mobile 390px — the site was designed mobile-first.
Don't
  • Don't use Jost, Lora, Playfair, or any other serif. Cormorant Garamond only.
  • Don't use more than one primary CTA button per viewport.
  • Don't place body text at full desktop width — it breaks warmth and readability.
  • Don't use Cormorant below 14px — use Cormorant 500 uppercase at small sizes only.
  • Don't use dusk blue as body text on white — contrast is too low.
  • Don't use gradients. Section transitions are always via wave SVG or background color blocks.
  • Don't add drop shadows to text or cards. The aesthetic is flat, warm, and paper-like.
  • Don't use clinical or sterile language. This is a human practice — every word must feel human.
